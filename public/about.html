Our History

Origin & Alliance with LAMON

The **Musculoskeletal Oncology Support Foundation (MONSUF)** was carved out from the **Lagos Musculoskeletal Oncology Network (LAMON)**. LAMON is a national medical collective formed by surgical oncologists, radiotherapists, pathologists, physiotherapists, and other healthcare practitioners who dedicate their careers to treating musculoskeletal tumors (sarcomas).

Members of LAMON meet on a monthly basis to review patient cases, evaluate treatments administered, and brainstorm clinical options to provide the best possible outcomes. In doing so, it became apparent that medical expertise alone is insufficient: patients face overwhelming financial barriers to accessing limb-salvage surgeries, chemotherapies, and prosthetics.

MONSUF was established with the primary aim of lifting this financial burden, providing critical social, emotional, and psychological support for sarcoma patients and their families, and funding oncology research and training.

Constitutional Mission

Aims & Objectives of MONSUF

  • Patient Social & Psychological Support

    To provide social and psychological support for patients with musculoskeletal tumours and their families throughout their clinical journey.

  • Encouraging Support Interactions

    To encourage interaction between individuals and organizations whose aim is to provide support for individuals with musculoskeletal tumours.

  • Improving Tumour Awareness

    To improve public awareness of musculoskeletal tumours, encouraging early detection, referral, and medical consultation.

  • Advancing Sarcoma Research

    To encourage and fund scientific research and clinical investigations into the causes, diagnostics, and surgical treatments of sarcomas.

  • Clinical Residency & Fellowship Training

    To support and fund the specialized training of medical residents, orthopedic surgeons, and nurses in musculoskeletal oncology care.
